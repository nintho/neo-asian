About Us






Our Story

Oanh Vo was born and raised in Saigon, Vietnam, where she learned to cook traditional Vietnamese dishes from her mother, including the beloved Pho. Her passion for cooking was further fueled by the diverse flavors of the neighboring countries like Cambodia and Thailand. Whose rich flavors inspired her to incorporate new ingredients and techniques into her culinary repertoire.


After moving to America and living on her own, Oanh found comfort in preparing her hometown dishes when she was feeling homesick. She soon realized that her cooking could bring joy to others who missed the flavors of home.


Pho is a popular Vietnamese dish that is renowned for its deep and complex flavors. The flavorful broth is made by simmering bones for up to 24 hours, extracting all the nutrients and flavors and creating a rich, nourishing base for the soup. To this, onions, ginger, and garlic are added, along with an array of spices such as cinnamon, star anise, coriander seeds, fennel seeds, bay leaves, cloves, and cardamom, which give it a distinct aroma and taste. These ingredients work together to create the hearty and comforting nature of the dish. The use of fresh and wholesome ingredients such as bean sprouts, basil, and lime wedges also adds a healthy element to the dish, making it a popular choice for those looking for a nutritious and satisfying meal. Overall, the combination of deep flavors from the spices, nourishing broth, and wholesome ingredients is what makes Pho a comforting and flavorful Vietnamese dish.
